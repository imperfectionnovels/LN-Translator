Worked examples (raw MT artifact → target prose). Names in brackets like [Name] are placeholders; the rules are novel-agnostic, do not read the placeholders as real characters:
- Said-bookism, unsupported tag upgrade. Source: 「滚！」李四说道。「我受够了，」他又道。 → "'Get out!' he said. 'I've had enough,' he said." NOT "exclaimed / declared / retorted": neutral 说道 / 道 does not specify the manner, so do not invent it for variety. Had the raw written 喊道 / 嘀咕 / 反驳, render it ("shouted / muttered / shot back"). Fidelity over variety.
- Calque, false-friend literal. "He could still hear the human voices below." → "He could still hear the chatter below." (人声 ≠ "human voices" in English; render as people's voices / chatter / talking.)
- Honorific flattening. "'I am not very good at this,' the disciple replied to the elder." → "'This disciple's abilities are limited,' he replied." (preserve the self-effacing honorific register; do not flatten to bare "I".)
- Inner thought via POV-pronoun shift (no quote marker). The raw marks inner monologue by a mid-paragraph shift from 他 / 她 to 我 / 吾 / 在下 with NO 「」/ 道 / 心想 tag. Source: 他翻动玉简，眉头紧锁。吾族劳作十代，怎能毁于一旦！ → "He turned the jade slip over, his brows knit tight. *My clan has labored ten generations. How could it be ruined in a single day!*" Italicize the first-person thought and its exclamation; do not leave a naked exclamation in third-person narration. Reasoning the raw keeps in 他 / 她 is free indirect discourse: leave it roman and third person, do not italicize it or rewrite it into an *I* thought.
- Register anachronism replaced. NOT: "the man's awesome glare almost stopped him from thinking." YES: "the man's fierce glare made his mind go blank for an instant." (drop "awesome" as a slangy intensifier.) But a discourse marker the source actually wrote is faithful: 说实话 → "Honestly,".
- Webnovel voice, not literal meme calque. 挂来！ (the POV character invoking his system cheat) → "Cheat power, do your thing!" or "Come on, cheat power!", NOT "Bring it on!" (he is invoking his cheat, not challenging an enemy). 溜了溜了！ → "I'm out, I'm out!", NOT "Slipping out, slipping out!".
- Idiom flavor without calque worship. 未雨绸缪 in strategic narration → "prepare before the storm" or "lay plans before the rain", NOT "binding the thatch before the rain" unless the rustic image is narratively useful.
- Locked titles never weld with 's. A long locked name (e.g. "Azure Void Treasure-Light Grotto-Heaven", or one already ending in 's like "True Person Storm's Edge") stays atomic. YES: "Azure Void Treasure-Light Grotto-Heaven was the grotto-heaven left behind by True Monarch [Name]." / "the confidence of True Person Storm's Edge firmed." NOT: "True Monarch [Name]'s Azure Void Treasure-Light Grotto-Heaven" or "True Person Storm's Edge's confidence". Recast with "left behind by / established by / the X of Y" or active voice; never shorten the name to dodge the pileup.
- Triple-appositive run-on split. NOT: "the dread that had taken hold of [Name], the grotto-heaven fragment, the fire-serpent born of Si-Fire, the urge to retreat, reversed all at once." YES: "the dread that had taken hold of [Name] reversed all at once: the grotto-heaven fragment, the fire-serpent born of Si-Fire, the urge to retreat, none of it weighed on him now." (Stacked nominal appositives between subject and verb leave the reader unable to track which item the verb attaches to; lift the verb out, then list the items with a colon.)
