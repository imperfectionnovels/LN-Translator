Worked examples (raw MT artifact → target prose). Names in brackets like [Name] are placeholders; the rules are novel-agnostic, do not read the placeholders as real characters:
- "[Name] knew that [Name]'s chance had finally come." → "[Name] knew his chance had finally come." (repeated name → pronoun)
- "He could not help but frown. He could not help but step back." → "He frowned, and despite himself took a step back." (vary the mechanical tic)
- "This matter, he had truly never considered." → "He had genuinely never considered this." (topic-comment fronting → natural English order)
- Cultivator title order. A personal name plus 真君 / 真人 / 上人 / 道人 renders title-first ("True Monarch [Name]", "True Person [Name]"); a descriptive epithet plus the same suffix renders epithet-first ("Demon-Purging True Person"). Pick one order per name and keep it every chapter.
- Said-bookism, unsupported tag upgrade. Source: 「滚！」李四说道。「我受够了，」他又道。「你会后悔的，」她道。 → "'Get out!' he said. 'I've had enough,' he said. 'You'll regret this,' she said." NOT: "exclaimed / declared / retorted". The source uses neutral 说道/道 and does not specify the manner; do not invent it for variety. If the raw HAD specified 喊道 / 嘀咕 / 反驳, render faithfully as "shouted / muttered / shot back." Prefer fidelity over variety: do not invent a manner the source did not mark.
- Calque, false-friend literal. "He could still hear the human voices below." → "He could still hear the chatter below." (人声 ≠ "human voices" in English; render as people's voices / chatter / talking)
- Calque, dangling participle. Sentence-initial "Looking out, the valley stretched for miles." → "From where he stood, the valley stretched for miles." ("Looking out," with no subject is a dangling participle in English; restructure or drop)
- Honorific flattening. "'I am not very good at this,' the disciple replied to the elder." → "'This disciple's abilities are limited,' he replied." (preserve self-effacing honorific register; do not flatten to bare "I")
- Fixed title preserved, carrier rewritten. Two long locked titles must not be welded with 's. Glossary: a long grotto-heaven name (e.g. "Azure Void Treasure-Light Grotto-Heaven") and the True Monarch who made it ("True Monarch [Name]"). YES: "Azure Void Treasure-Light Grotto-Heaven was the grotto-heaven left behind by True Monarch [Name]." NOT: "True Monarch [Name]'s Azure Void Treasure-Light Grotto-Heaven" (two long fixed titles welded with 's pileup). The locked title stays atomic; rewrite the carrier with "left behind by", "established by", "the X of Y".
- Possessive pileup on a glossary name ending in 's. Glossary: a locked name that already ends in 's, e.g. "True Person Storm's Edge". YES: "True Person Storm's Edge grew more confident." or "the confidence of True Person Storm's Edge firmed." NOT: "True Person Storm's Edge's confidence firmed." A glossary name that already contains 's never takes another 's; recast to "of [Name]" or use active voice; never shorten the name to dodge the pileup.
- Proper-noun title Title-Casing in prose AND in new_terms. A named spirit treasure "Cloud-Treading Skiff" (item category); a jade-slip manual title "Canon of Drifting Cloud and Bright Moon" (technique category; function words "of", "and" stay lowercase mid-title); 真人 → "True Person" / "True Persons" (rank, other category). NOT: "cloud-treading skiff", "canon of drifting cloud and bright moon", "true ones". These are proper nouns even when the source reads descriptive. Emit each in new_terms.en exactly as it should read in prose.
- Heavenly Stem × Five-Phase casing. Stems are Jia / Yi / Bing / Ding / Wu / Ji / Geng / Xin / Ren / Gui; Branches are Zi / Chou / Yin / Mao / Chen / Si / Wu / Wei / Shen / You / Xu / Hai. Phases are Wood / Fire / Earth / Metal / Water. Render compounds Title-Case on BOTH halves, joined by ASCII hyphen: 庚金灵仙 → "Geng-Metal spirit-immortal", 巳火 → "Si-Fire", 辰土 → "Chen-Earth", 甲木 → "Jia-Wood". NOT: "Geng-metal" / "Si-fire" / "Chen-earth" / "jia-Wood". One canonical form, every occurrence.
- Inner thought via POV-pronoun shift (no quote marker). The CN raw marks inner monologue by a shift from 他 / 她 / 他们 to 我 / 吾 / 我的 / 在下, mid-paragraph, with NO 「」 / "" / 道 / 想道 / 心想 tag. Source: 他翻动玉简，眉头紧锁。吾族劳作十代，怎能毁于一旦！ → "He turned the jade slip over, his brows knit tight. *My clan has labored ten generations. How could it be ruined in a single day!*" NOT: "He turned the jade slip over, his brows knit tight. My clan has labored ten generations. How could it be ruined in a single day!" (the second sentence is the POV character's inner voice; no speech tag, no quote glyph in the raw; italicize with *…*). Inner exclamation ("吾族…！", "不！", "好…！") gets italicized too; do not leave naked exclamation marks in third-person narration. Conversely, reasoning the raw keeps in the third person (他 / 她, no first-person break) is free indirect discourse: leave it roman and third person, do NOT italicize it or rewrite it into a first-person *I* thought.
- Comma-splice / three-clause run-on split. NOT: "That single name occupied only the smallest sliver of his memory from his previous life, had his cultivation not been deep enough, he would not have recalled it at all." YES: "That single name occupied only the smallest sliver of his memory from his previous life. Had his cultivation not been deep enough, he would not have recalled it at all." (split at the clause boundary; promote the conditional to a fronted hypothetical).
- Comma where a period belongs (do not downgrade a source sentence-terminal). NOT: "He held absolute confidence in this, it was, after all, a golden talent." YES: "He held absolute confidence in this. After all, it was a golden talent." (two independent clauses; the source 。 is a period in English, not a comma).
- Past-perfect chain compressed. NOT: "By then the man had recovered his peak Foundation Establishment cultivation, and had even refined a Blessed Land of his own, had found this very grotto-heaven fragment fallen here, and after refining it had been able to recover." YES: "By then the man had recovered his peak Foundation Establishment cultivation and refined a Blessed Land of his own. He must have found this grotto-heaven fragment fallen here and, after refining it, recovered." (set the prior-timeline anchor once with "had recovered… refined…"; switch to simple past for the elaboration).
- Mid-sentence paragraph break joined. NOT: "As a Geng-Metal spirit-immortal in this life, if he could nourish himself within it,\n\nthe benefits would be enormous!" YES: "As a Geng-Metal spirit-immortal in this life, if he could nourish himself within it, the benefits would be enormous!"
- 明明 simultaneity, not a past state. Source: 明明周围热浪滚滚，此刻却冰寒如冬。 YES: "The heat still rolled around them in waves, yet now the place was as cold as winter." NOT: "Only a moment before, the heat had been rolling around them, yet now it was cold as winter." (明明 …，此刻却 … is a present contrast, not a past tense).
- Register anachronism replaced. NOT: "To put it bluntly, the man's awesome glare almost stopped him from thinking." YES: "Plainly speaking, the man's fierce glare made his mind go blank for an instant." (drop modern-colloquial framing; drop "awesome" as slangy intensifier). But a sentence-opening discourse marker that the source actually wrote is faithful: 说实话 → "Honestly," is fine.
- Webnovel voice, not literal meme calque. Source: 挂来！ after the POV character trusts a system talent. YES: "Cheat power, do your thing!" or "Come on, cheat power!" NOT: "Bring it on!" (he is invoking his cheat, not challenging an enemy). Source: 溜了溜了！ YES: "I'm out, I'm out!" NOT: "Slipping out, slipping out!"
- Idiom flavor without calque worship. 未雨绸缪 in strategic narration → "prepare before the storm" or "lay plans before the rain." NOT: "binding the thatch before the rain" unless the rustic thatch image is narratively useful.
- Predicate preserved with a locked glossary name. Source: a chapter title "再遇[Name]" where [Name] is a locked character term. YES: "Encountering [Name] Again." NOT: "[Name] Once Again." A locked term plus an adverb is not a translation; the predicate (遇 / encounter / meet) must surface in the English too.
- Locative inversion. NOT: "in his hand appeared a pocket-sized golden vessel that gave off vast surges of aura." YES: "a pocket-sized golden vessel appeared in his hand, giving off vast surges of aura." English is subject-first.
- Triple-appositive run-on split. NOT: "Once the thought settled, the dread that had taken hold of [Name], the grotto-heaven fragment, the fire-serpent born of Si-Fire, the urge to retreat that had begun to stir in his heart, reversed all at once." YES: "Once the thought settled, the dread that had taken hold of [Name] reversed all at once: the grotto-heaven fragment, the fire-serpent born of Si-Fire, the urge to retreat stirring in his heart, none of it weighed on him now." (Three nominal appositives stacked between subject and verb leave the reader unable to track which item the verb attaches to; lift the verb out, then list the items with a colon).
- Intra-chapter terminology consistency before a term is locked. A Chinese term that has no glossary entry yet still recurs within the chapter: pick ONE English rendering on its first occurrence and use it for every later occurrence in the same chapter. NOT: "the superior-grade Spirit Treasure. Cloud-Treading Skiff!" followed two sentences later by "the marvelous power of this Spiritual Treasure was called 'Cloud-Treading,'". YES: "the superior-grade Spirit Treasure. Cloud-Treading Skiff!" then "the marvelous power of this Spirit Treasure was called 'Cloud-Treading,'". Spirit Treasure / Spiritual Treasure / Spiritual Item are not interchangeable variants; pick one on first use and report it in `new_terms` so it locks for later chapters.
