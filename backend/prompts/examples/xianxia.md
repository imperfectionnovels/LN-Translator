Worked examples. Some are raw MT artifact → target prose (what to avoid); others are positive source → literary-target models, each annotated with its single riskiest choice and why it still traces to the source. Names in brackets like [Name] are placeholders; the rules are novel-agnostic, do not read the placeholders as real characters:
- Said-bookism, unsupported tag upgrade. Source: 「滚！」李四说道。「我受够了，」他又道。 → "'Get out!' he said. 'I've had enough,' he said." NOT "exclaimed / declared / retorted": neutral 说道 / 道 does not specify the manner, so do not invent it for variety. Had the raw written 喊道 / 嘀咕 / 反驳, render it ("shouted / muttered / shot back"). Fidelity over variety.
- Calque, false-friend literal. "He could still hear the human voices below." → "He could still hear the chatter below." (人声 ≠ "human voices" in English; render as people's voices / chatter / talking.)
- Honorific flattening. "'I am not very good at this,' the disciple replied to the elder." → "'This disciple's abilities are limited,' he replied." (preserve the self-effacing honorific register; do not flatten to bare "I".)
- Locked titles never weld with 's. A long locked name (e.g. "Azure Void Treasure-Light Grotto-Heaven", or one already ending in 's like "True Person Storm's Edge") stays atomic. YES: "Azure Void Treasure-Light Grotto-Heaven was the grotto heaven left behind by True Monarch [Name]." / "the confidence of True Person Storm's Edge firmed." NOT: "True Monarch [Name]'s Azure Void Treasure-Light Grotto-Heaven" or "True Person Storm's Edge's confidence". Recast with "left behind by / established by / the X of Y" or active voice; never shorten the name to dodge the pileup.
- Positive model — atmosphere and a charged line, recomposed. Source: 云层翻涌，雷光在其间游走，像一条择人而噬的银龙。少年立于山巅，青衫被罡风扯得猎猎作响。他抬头，望着那一线即将劈落的天威，忽然笑了。「来吧，」他低声道，「我等这一日，等了很久了。」 → "The cloudbank roiled, and the lightning that moved inside it prowled like a silver dragon picking out its prey. On the summit the young man stood unmoving, his blue robe snapping in the gale. He looked up at the thin seam of heaven's wrath gathering to fall, and he smiled. 'Come, then,' he said, low. 'I have waited a long time for this day.'" What was done, and why it stays faithful: the long opening line is split for English breath; 游走 becomes "prowled" because the source's own simile (择人而噬, a dragon that picks prey to devour) already carries the predatory sense, so the connotation is rendered, not added; 天威 is given as "heaven's wrath" rather than the flatter "might" because the scene is a punishing tribulation and the menacing reading is the one the source carries here, the kind of tier-two choice a fidelity check should examine line by line; 道 stays "said" and 低声 rides as "low," not upgraded to "whispered." Nothing happens in the English that does not happen in the Chinese: the clouds, the lightning-dragon, the robe, the gale, the upward look, the smile, the spoken line, and the long wait are all in the source.
- Positive model — confrontation, register held, beat lifted. Source: 那老者眼皮都未抬，淡淡道：「就凭你们几个？」话音未落，周身气机陡然一沉，院中众人齐齐后退半步，无人再敢出声。 → "The old man did not even raise his eyelids. 'You few think you are enough?' he said, mild. The words were barely out when the pressure around him dropped all at once, and across the courtyard every one of them gave back half a step. No one spoke again." What was done: 淡淡 is held as "mild," not inflated to "coldly" or "contemptuously," so the menace comes from the content and the dropped pressure rather than from a loud tag; 就凭你们几个 becomes a real English challenge instead of a literal "just relying on you few"; the 气机一沉 → recoil → silence sequence keeps its exact order, because that order is the beat. No force is added: the contempt, the pressure, the collective half-step, and the silence are all the source's.
