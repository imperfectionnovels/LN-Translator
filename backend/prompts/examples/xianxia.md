Worked examples. Each isolates one rule. All names, titles, and treasures here are invented placeholders, not characters from any novel. The English wording is illustrative: imitate the shapes, never reuse these exact phrases.

- Neutral tag stays neutral: 「滚！」李四说道。 to "'Get out!' he said." NOT "exclaimed / declared": 说道 / 道 does not specify a manner, so do not invent one. Had the raw written 喊道 / 嘀咕, render it ("shouted" / "muttered"). Fidelity over variety.
- Frozen idiom renders as sense, never as image or simile: 倒抽一口凉气 to "drew in a sharp breath", not "sucked in a cold breath"; 心念一动 to "with a thought" ("with a thought, he summoned..."), not "his mind moved"; 雪上加霜 to "made things even worse", not "added frost on top of snow".
- Humble self-reference kept: 「我不太擅长这个，」弟子答道。 to "'This disciple's abilities are limited,' he replied." Not flattened to "I".
- Title frequency: 守岳真人没有回答。守岳真人抬起头看了他一眼，最终守岳真人摇了摇头。 to "Peak-Warding True Person did not answer. He looked up at him, and in the end he shook his head." The full title at its weight-bearing use; he / him between; never the full title in all three sentences.
- Title economy holds under action too: 扫尘真人挥剑斩下，扫尘真人随即后撤，扫尘真人再度逼近。 to "Dust-Sweeping True Person's sword came down. He fell back at once, then pressed in again." One full title carries the sequence; pronouns drive the motion; the title re-sounds only at the next weight-bearing beat.
- Locked names stay atomic, never welded with 's. YES: "Jade Hollow Grotto-Heaven was the grotto heaven left behind by True Monarch [Name]." / "the confidence of True Person Autumn's Edge firmed." NOT: "True Monarch [Name]'s Jade Hollow Grotto-Heaven" or "True Person Autumn's Edge's confidence". Recast with "the X of Y / left behind by" or active voice; never shorten the name to dodge the pileup. Between uses a pronoun or plain role noun ("he", "the old man") is not a shortening; the ban is on truncating or re-coining the label where it is used.
- An enumeration is one comma series, even charged, even mid-action: 剑宗，丹阁，百兽门，岂能少了我一席？ to "The Sword Sect, the Pill Pavilion, the Hundred Beast Gate, how could there be no seat for me among them?" Never chopped into fragments.
- Topic-comment takes a finite English frame, in thought as in narration: 以X杀人，如此巨大的冲突，Y岂能安然无恙？ to "He had killed with X. A clash that large, and Y was supposed to walk away clean?" NOT "Killing with X, a clash that large, how was Y supposed to walk away clean?" The same cut rescues a pileup: a sentence dragging three trailing modifiers gives the last one its own sentence.
- Recomposed atmosphere, faithful: 云层翻涌，雷光在其间游走，像一条择人而噬的银龙。少年立于山巅，青衫被罡风扯得猎猎作响。 to "The clouds churned, and lightning moved through them like a silver dragon picking out its prey. The young man stood on the summit. His blue robe snapped in the fierce wind." Subject and verb up front in every sentence; the simile is the source's own, so the verbs stay common; every image is the source's.
- A comma chain recomposes into English sentence shapes: 魔教倾巢而出，甚至连山门大阵都一并迁了过来，冲天的煞气几乎染红了半边天。 to "The Demon Sect had come out in full force, going so far as to move their mountain-gate grand formation along with them. The baleful aura surging skyward stained half the heavens red." NOT one English sentence chaining all three clauses with commas and "and": the sentence count and clause order serve the English; the meaning, tone, and every image stay the source's.
- Cohesion at seams: a cue folds or fronts, never the same anchor twice running. 「够了。」 / 闻言，众人一静。 / 此时，殿外传来脚步声。 to "'Enough.' / The crowd went quiet at that. / Then footsteps sounded outside the hall." NOT "At these words, the crowd went quiet. At this moment, footsteps sounded outside the hall.": stacked anchors read as scaffolding; vary the wording and the position, keep the relation.
