Worked examples. Some are MT artifact to target prose (what to avoid); others are source to literary-target models. Names in brackets like [Name] are placeholders, not real characters.
- Said-bookism: 「滚！」李四说道。 to "'Get out!' he said." NOT "exclaimed / declared": neutral 说道 / 道 does not specify the manner, so do not invent it. Had the raw written 喊道 / 嘀咕, render it ("shouted / muttered"). Fidelity over variety.
- Calque, false friend: "He could still hear the human voices below." to "He could still hear the chatter below." (人声 is people's voices / chatter, not "human voices".)
- Honorific register: 「我不太擅长这个，」弟子答道。 to "'This disciple's abilities are limited,' he replied." (keep the self-effacing register; do not flatten to "I".)
- Locked titles never weld with 's. A long locked name ("Azure Void Treasure-Light Grotto-Heaven", or one already ending in 's like "True Person Storm's Edge") stays atomic. YES: "Azure Void Treasure-Light Grotto-Heaven was the grotto heaven left behind by True Monarch [Name]." / "the confidence of True Person Storm's Edge firmed." NOT: "True Monarch [Name]'s Azure Void Treasure-Light Grotto-Heaven" or "True Person Storm's Edge's confidence". Recast with "left behind by / the X of Y" or active voice; never shorten the name to dodge the pileup. Between uses, a pronoun or a plain role noun ("he", "the old man") is not a shortening; the ban is on truncating or re-coining the label where it is used.
- Title frequency: the raw repeats a full title where English uses a pronoun. Source: 镇河真人没有回答。镇河真人抬起头看了他一眼，最终镇河真人摇了摇头。 to "River-Calming True Person did not answer. He looked up at him, and in the end he shook his head." NOT the full title in all three sentences. At its weight-bearing uses (first appearance in the scene, formal address, a status beat) the title appears whole; between them English refers with he / him / the True Person.
- Positive model (atmosphere, recomposed). Source: 云层翻涌，雷光在其间游走，像一条择人而噬的银龙。少年立于山巅，青衫被罡风扯得猎猎作响。他抬头，望着那一线即将劈落的天威，忽然笑了。「来吧，」他低声道，「我等这一日，等了很久了。」 to "The clouds churned, and lightning moved through them like a silver dragon picking out its prey. The young man stood on the summit without moving. His blue robe snapped in the fierce wind. He looked up at the heavenly might that was about to come crashing down, and suddenly he smiled. 'Come, then,' he said in a low voice. 'I have waited a long time for this day.'" Why it stays faithful: every sentence puts its subject and verb up front and lets the rest trail; the source's own simile (择人而噬, a dragon that picks prey) carries the menace, so the verbs stay common; 道 stays "said", 低声 is "in a low voice". Every image is the source's.
- Positive model (confrontation, register held). Source: 那老者眼皮都未抬，淡淡道：「就凭你们几个？」话音未落，周身气机陡然一沉，院中众人齐齐后退半步，无人再敢出声。 to "The old man didn't even raise his eyelids. 'You few think you are enough?' he said mildly. The words were barely out when the pressure around him suddenly sank, and everyone in the courtyard retreated half a step at once. No one dared to make a sound again." Why it stays faithful: 淡淡 held as "said mildly", not inflated to "coldly", so the menace comes from the dropped pressure, not a loud tag; the 气机一沉 to recoil to silence order is kept, because that order is the beat; subject and verb stay up front in every sentence. No force added.
- Coordinated list kept as a comma series, not chopped into fragments. Source: 剑宗，丹阁，万兽门，岂能少了我一席？ to "The Sword Sect, the Pill Pavilion, the Myriad Beast Gate, how could there be no seat for me among them?" NOT: "The Sword Sect. The Pill Pavilion. The Myriad Beast Gate. How could there be no seat for me?" (a run of parallel names is one enumeration: it stays a comma series even in a charged line; the short-beat action license does not reach it).
