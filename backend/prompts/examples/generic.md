Worked examples (raw MT artifact → target prose):
- Said-bookism resisted. Source: 「闭嘴。」他说道。「我受不了了。」他又说。 → "'Be quiet,' he said. 'I can't stand it,' he said again." NOT: "exclaimed / declared / retorted." (do not upgrade a bare neutral tag to a marked one for variety).
- Idiom kept at its locked rendering, not re-paraphrased. Glossary (idiom category): 画蛇添足 → "gild the lily". Source: 这么做无异于画蛇添足 → "Doing so was no better than gilding the lily." NOT a fresh paraphrase each time it recurs ("adding feet to a snake", "overdoing things"). An idiom carries its established English rendering and keeps it every time, the same as a locked term; its sense is never dropped or freely reworded.
- Aspect-marker tense overreach avoided. Source: 他走进了大厅，看见了那位老人 → "He walked into the hall and saw the old man." NOT: "He had walked into the hall and had seen the old man." (CN aspect marker 了 does not require past perfect in English; routine prior-action sequencing is simple past).
- Comma preserved, not period-split. Source: 他举起长剑，剑身泛起一层寒光，缓缓指向对手。 → "He raised his sword, its blade taking on a cold sheen, and leveled it slowly at his opponent." NOT: "He raised his sword. The blade took on a cold sheen. He leveled it slowly at his opponent." (one continuous action: keep it as a single flowing sentence with subordination and a coordinator, rather than chopping every comma into a full stop. A comma splice is only two INDEPENDENT clauses with no coordinator; commas joining a compound sentence, a subordinate clause, an appositive, or a series are correct English and stay).
