Worked examples (raw MT artifact → target prose):
- Sworn-brother address preserved. Source: "大哥，今日的事不能就这么算了。" → "Big Brother, this cannot end here today." NOT: "Hey, bro, we can't let this go." (preserve the sworn-brother formality; "Big Brother" is a relationship marker, not biological).
- Martial technique name treated as proper noun. Source: 他使出降龙十八掌中的"亢龙有悔" → "He struck with Regret of the Soaring Dragon, the first of the Eighteen Dragon-Subduing Palms." (named technique stays Title-Case; the rendering is poetic-formal).
- Internal energy ≠ cultivation qi. Source: 他催动内力，一掌将石板震碎。 → "He gathered his internal energy and shattered the stone slab with a single palm strike." NOT: "He cultivated his spiritual qi" (this is wuxia internal-power, not xianxia cultivation).
- Light-body skill rendered literally. Source: 他施展轻功，一跃而上屋顶 → "He used his light-body skill and leapt onto the rooftop." NOT: "He parkoured up to the roof."
- Stock declaration kept literal. Source: "你今日休想活着离开！" → "You shall not leave here alive today!" NOT: "You're not getting out of this." (preserve the stagey period register).
- Jianghu reputation reaction. Source: 围观众人见状，纷纷议论 → "The watching crowd murmured among themselves." (preserve the crowd-reaction beat; reputation matters in wuxia plotting).
- Acupoint sealing per glossary. Source: 他出手点中对手的麻穴 → "He struck the opponent's numbing acupoint." NOT: "He hit a pressure point."
- Fight choreography rendered move by move. Source: 他左脚一错，侧身避过当胸一剑，反手一掌削向对方手腕；对方撤剑回防，两人各退一步。 → "He shifted his left foot and twisted aside from the thrust aimed at his chest, then on the backhand cut a palm toward the other man's wrist. His opponent drew the sword back to guard, and the two of them each gave a step." NOT: "They traded a flurry of blows." (preserve the geometry of the exchange, step / evade / counter / parry / disengage; the reader should be able to follow the fight, not just be told it happened).
- Self-effacing jianghu honorific kept. Source: 「依在下之见，此事另有隐情。」 → "'As this humble one sees it, there is more to this matter than it appears.'" NOT: "'If you ask me, something else is going on.'" (preserve the self-effacing register; do not flatten "在下" to a casual modern "I").
- Register varies by social class within one scene. Sect master, measured indirection: 「阁下既已至此，想必心中早有计较。」 → "'Since you have come this far, you have surely already weighed the matter in your heart.'" Tavern keeper, working-class colloquial: 「客官，打尖还是住店？」 → "'What'll it be, guest, a bite to eat or a bed for the night?'" (a sect master and a tavern keeper do not share a register; do not level both to neutral modern English).
