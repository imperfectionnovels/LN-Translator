GENRE: same-sex romance, covering Japanese yaoi / shounen-ai / yuri and Chinese danmei (耽美) / baihe (百合). The throughline is a romance between two characters of the same gender; the setting (modern, period, fantasy, xianxia) shapes the register. Only one overlay loads per novel, so this file inlines the period-CN cultivation essentials it most often needs; for any other setting, apply that setting's naming conventions and keep this file's romance register.

Narrative mode: close third person most often (first person supported), frequently dual-POV alternating between the two leads. Match the source.

Target prose: emotionally interior, restrained on the body, generous on the moment. The genre prizes slow-burn intimacy, the held glance, the half-finished sentence, the hand left on a sleeve, so render small gestures with weight rather than flattening micro-moments into summary. A third-person narrator stays very close to one POV, inside the longing, not editorializing from outside. Interior thought, the protagonist's noticing (the curve of a wrist, a hand on a teacup), is the genre; do not rush it.

Body and heat: render the gradient precisely, where a held wrist outweighs a kiss and a kiss outweighs sex, and pick the exact modifier ("barely there" versus "lingering" versus "deliberate"). Match the source's heat level exactly, neither raised nor lowered, avoiding both the porn-ish lurch and the prudish euphemism. Use he / she / they per the source's framing; in a cross-dressing or hidden-identity arc, keep the source's pronoun for the disguise period and switch only when it switches.

Honorific shifts ARE the romance arc: preserve every form per the chapter's stage and never collapse the progression ("Shen-shixiong to Shen-ge to Shen Qing" across 200 chapters is the arc). Hedged, indirect, formal address persisting between intimates is the heat itself.

Period / cultivation setting (the genre's most common pairing, inlined since the xianxia overlay does not load here):
- Self-effacing first person renders per source ("this disciple" 弟子, "this humble one" 在下, "this junior" 晚辈; "this one" 妾身 female-formal only); titled second person renders per source (Senior Brother / Junior Sister / Master / Sect Master / Dao Lord / Fellow Daoist).
- A role label is Title Case when it names one specific character (Senior Brother Shen, Master, Sect Master [Name]) and lowercase when generic or plural (the inner disciples, the elders).
- Cultivator titles on 真人 / 真君 render title-first for a name and epithet-first for an epithet; realm names take their English forms (Foundation Establishment, Golden Core, Nascent Soul), never transliterated; generic cultivation vocabulary (qi, dao, karma) stays sentence-case; Stem and Branch compounds are Title-Cased on both halves with a hyphen (Bing-Fire, Geng-Metal).
- Brotherhood terms (shidi, shixiong, shifu) shift meaning once the romance kindles, the same "younger brother" now carrying weight. Hold that.

Setting-specific vocabulary, per glossary (often surfacing in chapter titles and author notes): CN danmei "shou" (受) / "gong" (攻), "black-bellied" (黑腹), "scum gong" (渣攻), "white lotus" (白莲花), mood tags 甜宠 / 虐 / 糖 / 刀, fandom "cp" / "ship"; JP "seme" / "uke", archetypes tsundere / yandere / kuudere. Preserve these, often romanized.

Watch for: straight-washing (translate same-sex as same-sex, keeping the pronouns and relationship vocabulary); modernizing period-CN romance (the first hundred chapters of formal address are the seduction); and explaining the cultural register in narration (if the source assumes the reader knows "shixiong" or "scum gong", so does the target).
