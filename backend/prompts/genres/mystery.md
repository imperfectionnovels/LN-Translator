GENRE: mystery / detective fiction. Investigation drives the plot: whodunit, police procedural, thriller, suspense, courtroom, locked-room, supernatural mystery. The reader is co-investigator, so what is withheld matters as much as what is revealed.

Narrative mode: close third person (often the detective) or first person, past tense. The POV controls what the reader knows; preserve the source's exact calibration of disclosure.

Target prose: precise, observational, tight. Clues only land if rendered accurately, and red herrings only work rendered straight. Match the source's specific narrator: sardonic (Chandler), clinical (procedural), or genteel (cozy).

Clue fidelity is critical, because physical detail is evidence:
- Keep exact spatial relationships: "the knife was on the table by the lamp" does not soften to "near the lamp".
- Keep timestamps, distances, measurements, and weights as exact numerals and units; render a range as a range ("around 9:15 to 9:20") and a precision as precision ("exactly 9:17").
- Keep a witness's exact phrasing, including hedges: "I think I saw him leave" does not strengthen to "I saw him leave". The phrasing is what is being analyzed.
- Keep color, material, and size exact.

Characters lie: render a lie in the speaker's voice as if true ("I was home all evening"), never editorialized toward the truth the detective will later prove. Render a red herring straight, without extra suspicion-loaded wording, and do not soften a genuinely incriminating detail into innocent prose. Do not clarify an ambiguity the source preserved (if a witness says "a man" and the gender is being kept open, keep "a man"), and do not add a tell (avoided eye contact, tapping fingers) the source did not give.

Dialogue: render interrogation back-and-forth precisely, since single words carry legal weight; preserve the shape of testimony (what a witness emphasizes, brushes past, or gets out of order); keep false starts and disfluencies ("I, I mean, what I meant was"). In the reveal, preserve the detective's point-by-point pacing and any false solution at full strength, with no proleptic skepticism, because the cumulative weight is the storytelling.

Vocabulary: match the source's jurisdiction per glossary (公安局 "Public Security Bureau", 派出所 "local police station", 法医 "forensic examiner"; 警視庁 "Metropolitan Police Department"), not domesticated to "DA / precinct" unless the glossary chose it. Keep forensic precision: "stab wound" versus "incised wound", "blunt-force trauma" versus "skull fracture". Match the sub-genre's atmosphere: cozy warmth, hardboiled terseness, procedural method, the restrained uncanny of supernatural mystery, the compressed pacing of a thriller.
