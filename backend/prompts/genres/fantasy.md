GENRE: Western-style fantasy. Secondary-world fiction with magic systems, mythical creatures, kingdoms, knights, and sorcerers; European medieval or early-modern reference points (Arthurian, Norse, Tolkienian). The protagonist is native to the world. Not isekai (no transmigrator), not xianxia (no qi or Dao), not wuxia. A CN/JP/KR web novel that adopts these conventions wholesale (a knight in a magic academy, dragons hoarding gold) is this genre.

Narrative mode: third-person limited or omniscient, occasionally first; omniscient still switches heads only at breaks, never mid-paragraph. The narrator is elevated but contemporary, the register of modern published fantasy (McKillip, Jemisin, Hobb, Le Guin), neither pseudo-medieval pastiche nor modern slang. Characters speak to their station: commoners plain, nobles formal, mages technical about their craft, clergy with liturgical cadence. Interior thought matches a character's education, a court mage reasoning differently from a village blacksmith.

Target prose: elevated standard English. Render formal address and oath language with weight, without forsoothery ("thou", "verily", "milord") unless the source itself is being deliberately archaic.

Magic vocabulary: spell names, schools, and named concepts are Title-Cased proper nouns ("Fireball", "School of Evocation", "the Weave"); tier names (Apprentice / Adept / Archmage) are titles; quasi-Latin incantations render in italics with the words intact ("Lumos!"); the source's energy term (mana / essence / vis) stays consistent and distinct (do not collapse "essence" into "magic" if the source separates them). Creatures keep standard English forms (dragons, elves, dwarves; dwarves rather than dwarfs when Tolkien-coded), and invented creatures or named familiars are proper nouns ("Heartstone Wyrm").

Titles and names: "Your Grace" (dukes), "Your Majesty" (kings and queens), "Your Highness" (princes and princesses), "my lord / my lady", "Sir / Dame", "Brother / Sister" for clergy or order members. Knightly orders keep their full name and determiner ("the Order of the Silver Dawn"); royal forms use Roman regnal numerals ("Aldric III"). Kingdoms, cities, fortresses, and regions are proper nouns ("the Spine of the World", "the Northern Reaches"). Weapon types are common nouns (longsword, halberd, mace); named weapons are proper nouns ("Longclaw"). Combat is visceral, not video-gamey ("the edge caught the orc's pauldron and bit into the joint beneath", never "Slash for 15 damage"). Pantheons are proper nouns, and oaths keep their cadence ("By the Maker's hand").

Watch for: court etiquette under-rendered (a bow, a kneeling, a removed helmet is a status performance, not a skippable stage direction); and modernized dialogue (a knight does not say "no way"). If the source slips cultivation verbs (修炼 / 突破) into a fantasy world, render the nearest fantasy concept ("practice", "breakthrough") and treat it as anomalous.
