GENRE: isekai / transmigration / reincarnation novels. A protagonist from the contemporary modern world (often Earth, sometimes a near-future or science-fictional version) ends up in a different world: fantasy, historical-China-with-magic, dating sim, novel-world, video game. Mechanics often borrow from RPGs: stat sheets, classes, levels, skill trees, system notifications. Tone runs from gritty survival to cozy slice-of-life-after-transmigration to comedic meta-commentary.

Narrative mode: close third-person OR first-person, depending on source. The POV is usually the transmigrator: a modern consciousness in an unfamiliar setting.

Target prose: contemporary register grounded by genre conventions. The transmigrator's interior voice is modern; the world they have arrived in is whatever it is. The contrast IS the genre.

Voice register:
- Narrator (when third): close to the transmigrator's modern sensibility. Observational about the new world without becoming a tour guide. The reader learns the setting through the POV's reactions, not exposition.
- External dialogue: world-appropriate. If the transmigrator arrives in a medieval fantasy, the locals speak in slightly elevated register; if they arrive in an office dating-sim, contemporary. Match the world's register, not the POV's.
- Internal thought: this is the most distinctive isekai voice. Modern, often sarcastic, often gaming-literate, often a step ahead of the local characters because the POV has read this novel / played this game / lived in the modern world. Render with controlled snap.

System notifications:
- System-interface formatting follows base.md (bold 【...】 lines, ALL-CAPS where the source uses it); the genre cases below apply it to status windows.
- "【Status Window】", "【Quest Accepted: Defeat the Demon King】", "【+5 EXP】", "【Skill: Fireball acquired】". Title-Case the field name, render values per glossary.
- If the source uses ALL-CAPS for system text, preserve it (rare). If the source uses 【...】 with prose inside, render bold with 【...】 brackets preserved.
- Numerical stats and percentages stay as numerals. "HP 500 / 500" stays "HP 500 / 500."

Class, skill, and item names:
- Render as proper nouns, Title-Cased. "Berserker," "Light Mage," "Healer," "Fireball," "Holy Sword Excalibur." Function words ("of", "the", "and") stay lowercase mid-name.
- Skill descriptions often appear in italicized infodump blocks. Preserve the italics. Stats lists (Power +50, Range: 30m) preserve the format.
- "Original work" / "in the novel" / "in the game" (原作 / 小说里 / 游戏里): the POV's metacommentary. Translate naturally; the meta-awareness is genre-essential.

Transmigration genre vocabulary:
- "Transmigrator" (穿越者), "regressor" (returnee / 回归者), "original body" (原主), "novel-world" / "this world" / "the world of [Novel Title]": render per glossary.
- "I should have died" / "Wait, where am I?" / "This is... the novel I read last week!": typical opening beats. Render the disorientation faithfully; do not flatten the POV's confusion.
- "Cheat" / "system" / "skill" / "status": these are class-of-thing terms borrowed from gaming culture. Translate per glossary; "system" is usually preserved as "system" rather than "the System" unless capitalized in source.

Power-scaling and progression:
- Level / EXP / class up: translate per glossary, preserve numerals.
- "Plot armor" / "main character privilege": when the POV invokes these as self-aware framing, render the gaming idiom faithfully (the POV character genuinely thinks in these terms).

Stock declarations:
- "Wait, I know this scene!" / "This is the part where [X happens]!" / "Don't tell me... this is the [boss name]?!"
- Render with the POV's gamer / reader excitement intact. The meta-recognition is the joke.

Two register modes to switch between:
1. Modern interior voice (POV character thinking in modern Japanese / Chinese / Korean idiom): contemporary, may use gaming vocabulary, may be sarcastic.
2. World-appropriate exterior (POV character speaking with locals): match the world's register. The POV might think "wow, what a tropey villain monologue" while saying aloud "I shall not yield to your tyranny." Both registers are correct in their slot.

Watch-list:
- Do NOT modernize the world's exterior dialogue to match the POV's interior voice. The medieval guard does not say "dude."
- Do NOT westernize transmigration vocabulary. "Isekai" stays "isekai" if locked in glossary; "regression" stays "regression"; "novel transmigration" stays its form.
- Honorifics: if the world is Japanese-coded (Asuka-san, Yuki-chan), preserve them; if Chinese-coded (Big Sister Mei), use the Chinese family-address vocabulary; if Western-coded, render with English titles. Match the world.
- Stats and numbers stay as numerals; do not spell out. "HP 500" not "five hundred hit points."
