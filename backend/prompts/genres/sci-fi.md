GENRE: science fiction. Speculative fiction grounded, at least nominally, in technology or science: hard SF, space opera, cyberpunk, mecha, near-future thriller, post-apocalyptic, alien-contact, time-travel. The differentiator from fantasy is that the speculative elements are framed as physics, not magic.

Narrative mode: varies by sub-genre, so match the source rather than flattening it: hard SF leans omniscient or limited with a technical voice, cyberpunk first-person or close-third street vernacular, space opera cinematic multi-POV. Omniscient breadth means ranging across scenes at breaks, never head-hopping inside a paragraph.

Target prose: precise. Technical jargon is a feature when the source uses it and stays consistent across the novel. The narrator's register matches the sub-genre (dry and exacting hard SF, clipped noir cyberpunk, sweeping space opera). Characters speak from their training: engineers in units and failure modes, soldiers in chain-of-command and callsigns, hackers in handles and exploits. Interior thought is technical when the character is: a pilot under fire tracks the bandit at their seven and their fuel state, not the beauty of the stars.

Speculative vocabulary, preserved consistently: drive names (warp, jump, slipstream, fold), ship classes (frigate, destroyer, battlecruiser), weapons (railgun, plasma cannon, mass driver), augmentations (cyberware, neural lace, exocortex), corporations (megacorp, zaibatsu). A "psyker" stays a psyker, not "esper" in chapter 1 and "telepath" in chapter 12. Keep cyberpunk slang (chrome, ice, jack-in, sprawl, ripperdoc) in-genre, not modernized to plain words.

Units and protocol stay precise: metric stays metric, SI prefixes and abbreviations intact ("12 GW", "0.8 c", "3 AU"), never spelled out; technical numerals as numerals ("throttle to 80%"); scientific notation where the source uses it. Preserve callsigns as proper nouns ("Viper Lead", "Bravo Six"), radio brevity (Roger, Wilco, Affirmative, Negative, Over) in standard register, full military rank, and ALL-CAPS klaxon or system text ("HULL BREACH DETECTED"). Render an AI's voice cold where the source frames it cold; do not warm it into an emotional register.

Apply the sub-genre register the source signals: cyberpunk runs to short fragments and intact future-trademark vocabulary; space opera is cinematic, wide-shot before close-shot, with fleet geometry and full noble or military titles preserved; hard SF treats speculative concepts as physics (render a Krasnikov tube's explanation faithfully, do not handwave) and keeps math intact ("E = γmc²").
